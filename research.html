Research

Publications, service and academic foundations.

The research strand of my career sits behind every production system I have built. Its questions are about how to make good decisions when several criteria matter and when information is incomplete. Its venues are the leading conferences in artificial intelligence. Its outputs continue to inform how I work today.

Research focus

Multi-objective decision making under uncertainty.

My doctoral research at the National University of Ireland, Cork, supervised within the Cork Constraint Computation Centre and funded by the IRCSET / IBM Enterprise Partnership Scheme, sat in one of three streams of IBM's Risk Management Colloboratory project. The central question I worked on was this: in a setting where a decision maker has several competing objectives, but cannot or will not write down a single utility function that combines them, how can an algorithm still produce solutions that respect their preferences, and how can we elicit just enough information about those preferences to make the search tractable?

The thesis line of work produced exact and approximate algorithms for multi-objective constraint optimisation and influence diagrams with preference information, and the experimental side of the work compared those algorithms against the dominant techniques of the time. The collaborations with IBM Research Labs Dublin and colleagues in the Insight Centre extended the work to lexicographic and weighted-average preference models and to interactive elicitation methods that minimised the trade-off information required from a human decision maker.

During my Science Foundation Ireland Post-Doctoral Fellowship at the Insight Centre for Data Analytics (2014 to 2017), the work broadened into probabilistic graphical models, clustering and influence diagrams for industrial decision problems, as part of a programme run with United Technology Research Centre.
