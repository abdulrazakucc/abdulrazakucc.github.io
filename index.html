About

Bridging probabilistic AI research and production ML.

I am a Senior Data Scientist at Extreme Networks, building generative-AI and machine-learning systems that diagnose, troubleshoot, and optimise enterprise wireless networks at scale. My current focus is multi-modal AI for network telemetry, autonomous agent workflows that combine GenAI with classical ML, and preference-driven personalisation of networking solutions — work that has reduced wireless-issue investigation time from hours to seconds across thousands of access points and switches.

My research roots are in probabilistic graphical models, multi-objective decision making under uncertainty, and preference elicitation — published at AAAI, IJCAI, UAI, CP and ICTAI in collaboration with IBM Research Labs Dublin and United Technology Research Centre. Across industry and academia I have co-invented nine granted US patents spanning intrusion detection, alarm classification, and root-cause analysis.

The thread connecting all of it: I like problems where the data is messy, the signal is weak, and a decision still has to be made. That is what probabilistic AI is for — and increasingly, what well-grounded GenAI is for too. I enjoy the engineering work of taking ideas from a notebook to a production pipeline that ships, scales, and stays correct.

What I bring

Four threads I weave through every project.

01

Probabilistic thinking by default

A decade of probabilistic graphical models, Bayesian inference and PyMC3 means I model uncertainty explicitly instead of pretending it isn’t there — which matters for any system that needs to make a call when the data is incomplete or noisy.

02

GenAI grounded in domain signal

LLM reasoning combined with classical ML, statistical models and knowledge graphs. I build agents that act on telemetry, not chatbots that summarise it — the difference shows up in alert quality and false-positive rate.

03

Research-to-production fluency

Papers at AAAI / IJCAI / UAI on one side, PySpark / PyFlink pipelines on AWS and Azure on the other. I can prototype a novel approach and then ship the version that survives production constraints.

04

Multi-objective decision making

The PhD focus that quietly informs everything: framing trade-offs explicitly, eliciting preferences, and finding solutions that satisfy multiple stakeholders. Underrated skill in any cross-functional ML team.
