Context

How these patents came about.

Eight of the nine patents above were co-invented during my time at Johnson Controls (2017 to 2020) and one is the outcome of earlier research collaboration with IBM colleagues during my doctoral work. The patents are not the work itself; they are a record of the parts of the work that were sufficiently novel to be protected and sufficiently useful to be worth protecting.

I tend to think of patents the way I think of publications: as a way of putting an idea on the public record in a form that other people can build on. The themes above (decision intelligence, alarm management and building security analytics) reflect the kinds of industrial problems I worked on during that period, and they sit alongside the more recent generative-AI and wireless-networking work I describe in the Practice section of this site.
