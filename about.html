About

The journey behind the work.

A career shaped by a love of mathematics, an attraction to the messy edges of real-world data, and the conviction that good engineering and good research belong to the same craft.

A short biography

I am a Staff Data Scientist at Extreme Networks, where I lead the design of generative-AI and machine-learning systems that diagnose and optimise enterprise wireless networks at scale. My current focus is on multi-modal AI for network telemetry, autonomous agent workflows that combine GenAI with classical machine learning, and the preference-driven personalisation of networking solutions for customers operating at many sizes and complexities.

My route here was not linear. I began as a lecturer in mathematics at Osmania University in Hyderabad, teaching abstract and linear algebra, real and numerical analysis, and probability and statistics to undergraduate students for five years. That grounding in formal mathematics is the quiet foundation underneath everything I have done since. It is the reason I am comfortable defining a problem precisely, reasoning about convergence and complexity, and writing down a model before reaching for a library.

In 2010 I moved to Cork, Ireland to begin a PhD in Artificial Intelligence at the National University of Ireland, Cork as part of the IBM Risk Management Colloboratory project, funded by the IRCSET / IBM Enterprise Partnership Scheme. My research was on multi-objective decision making under uncertainty, with a particular focus on how to elicit and reason about user preferences when stakeholders disagree about what matters most. The work produced exact and approximate algorithms that took preference information into account, and was published at AAAI, IJCAI, UAI, CP and ICTAI in collaboration with IBM Research Labs Dublin.

I stayed in Cork for the next thirteen years. After the PhD I held a Science Foundation Ireland Post-Doctoral Fellowship at the Insight Centre for Data Analytics, working on probabilistic graphical models, clustering and influence diagrams as part of a United Technology Research Centre machine-learning programme. From 2017 I moved into industry as a Data Scientist at Johnson Controls, where I built supervised, unsupervised and probabilistic models for intrusion detection, access control and retail analytics. The eight US patents I co-invented during that period reflect the breadth of that work, from Pareto-optimal alarm classification to building security analytics with site-independent signature generation.

In 2020 I joined Extreme Networks, first in Cork and then in the United States. The pivot from traditional industrial systems to wireless networking opened a new application surface for the same probabilistic and decision-theoretic ideas I had been working with for a decade. With the arrival of capable foundation models, the work has expanded to include generative AI grounded in domain signal, where large language model reasoning sits inside an architecture that also contains statistical models, knowledge graphs and real-time telemetry from devices in the field.

What draws me to this work

The thread connecting everything I have done is a fondness for problems where the data is messy, the signal is weak, and a decision still has to be made. That is what probabilistic AI is for, and it is increasingly what well-grounded generative AI is for too. I enjoy the craft of taking an idea from a notebook to a production pipeline that ships, scales and stays correct, and I enjoy the patience required to model uncertainty explicitly instead of pretending it is not there.

I value collaborations in which engineers, scientists and product people argue about trade-offs in the open, because the work always benefits from it. I am happiest when an interesting research question can be turned into a useful product capability, and I have spent most of my career trying to make that translation feel less rare than it usually is.

Outside the work

I read and speak English, Hindi, Telugu and Urdu. I was born in Hyderabad and naturalised as an Irish citizen in 2021 after thirteen years in Cork. I am married, and now live in the United States. I retain a fondness for the mathematics I used to teach, and I still think undergraduate real analysis is one of the most beautifully constructed subjects in the curriculum.
